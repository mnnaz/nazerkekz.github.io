Computational Analysis of Emotional Language and
Female Representation in Disney Animated Films

Abstract

This study explores emotional language and female representation in Disney animated films through computational text analysis. The project examines five protagonists: Snow White, Cinderella, Mulan, Elsa, and Mirabel.

The results reveal a historical shift from romantic femininity toward identity, autonomy, and emotional complexity.

Introduction

Disney female protagonists have significantly influenced cultural representations of femininity across generations. Earlier heroines were associated with romantic idealism and emotional softness, while modern protagonists increasingly emphasize independence, identity, and psychological complexity.

Research Question

How has emotional language and female representation evolved in Disney female protagonists over time?
