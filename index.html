Introduction

The Golden Man is one of the most remarkable archaeological discoveries in Kazakhstan. Found in 1969 near Almaty, this Saka warrior buried in gold has become a national symbol of pride, history, and independence.

Timeline

5th century BCE

A young Saka warrior is buried in the Issyk burial mound, wearing golden armor.

1969

The burial is discovered by archaeologist Kemal Akishev.

1991

After Kazakhstan's independence, the Golden Man becomes a national symbol.

1997

Independence Monument in Almaty features the Golden Man at the top.

2014–2023

The Golden Man travels the world as part of international exhibitions.

Legacy

Today, the Golden Man is a symbol of Kazakhstan’s spirit. His image appears on banknotes, monuments, and in museums, reminding us of the country’s deep historical roots and cultural richness.
