Computational Analysis of Emotional Language and Female Representation in Disney Animated Films

A Digital Humanities project exploring emotional language, sentiment analysis, and female representation in Disney animated films.

About the Project

This project examines the evolution of emotional language and female representation in Disney animated films through computational text analysis. The analysis focuses on five protagonists: Snow White, Cinderella, Mulan, Elsa, and Mirabel. Using Python-based methods such as sentiment analysis, lexical frequency analysis, and thematic keyword analysis, the project explores how Disney heroines evolved from traditional romantic femininity toward identity, autonomy, emotional complexity, and interpersonal support.

Snow White

Traditional femininity, emotional softness, romantic dreaming, and domesticity.

Cinderella

Hope, fantasy, emotional patience, and romantic idealism.

Mulan

Honor, courage, resistance, responsibility, and identity.

Elsa

Emotional control, repression, self-acceptance, and independence.

Mirabel

Family belonging, emotional support, and collective identity.
